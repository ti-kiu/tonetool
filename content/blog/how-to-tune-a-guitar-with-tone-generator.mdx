## How to Tune by Ear

### Method 1: Direct Matching (Beginner)

1. Play the reference tone for the string you're tuning
2. Pluck the string
3. Listen for "beating" — a wobbly sound when two notes are close but not identical
4. Adjust the tuning peg until the beating slows and stops
5. The string is in tune when you hear **one clean tone**

### Method 2: Harmonic Matching (Advanced)

1. Play a harmonic at the 5th fret of the lower string
2. Play a harmonic at the 7th fret of the next higher string
3. These should be the same pitch
4. Adjust until they match perfectly

This works for: 6th→5th, 5th→4th, 4th→3rd, and 2nd→1st strings.

## Tips for Accurate Tuning

- **Use headphones** in a quiet room — ambient noise makes matching harder
- **Start with the 6th string** and work up — it's easier to hear low frequencies
- **Pluck near the 12th fret** for a clearer, more sustained tone
- **Let the note ring** for 2–3 seconds before adjusting — rushing causes overshooting

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Happens | Fix |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Tuning an octave off | Reference tone is E3 (164.81 Hz) instead of E2 (82.41 Hz) | Double-check the frequency |
| String breaks | Tuning too high | Always tune *up* to pitch, never guess above it |
| Sounds out of tune after tuning | Intonation issue on the guitar | Check 12th fret harmonic vs. fretted note |
